Seven ancient wonders of the world

Great Pyramid of Giza

The only one of the ancient wonders still standing today, it was built as a tomb for the Egyptian Pharaoh Khufu. It is located in Giza, Egypt.

Hanging Gardens of Babylon

Legend holds that these were a series of terraced gardens built by King Nebuchadnezzar II for his wife in ancient Mesopotamia. Their existence is still debated by historians.

Statue of Zeus at Olympia

A massive, 40-foot-tall seated figure of the god Zeus, crafted from ivory and gold by the Greek sculptor Phidias. It was housed in the Temple of Zeus in Olympia, Greece.

Temple of Artemis at Ephesus

A magnificent temple dedicated to the Greek goddess of the hunt, Artemis. It was located in the ancient city of Ephesus, in what is now Turkey.

Mausoleum at Halicarnassus

A monumental tomb built for Mausolus, a satrap in the Persian Empire, by his wife Artemisia. It was located in Halicarnassus, in modern-day Bodrum, Turkey.

Colossus of Rhodes

An enormous bronze statue of the sun god Helios, built at the harbor entrance of the city of Rhodes on the Greek island of the same name.

Lighthouse of Alexandria

Also known as the Pharos of Alexandria, this lighthouse was a towering structure that guided ships into the harbor of Alexandria, Egypt. It was one of the tallest man-made structures of its time.
