Indian National Symbols


Indian Flag


The Flag Code of India, 2002, and the Prevention of Insults to National Honour Act, 1971, govern the use and display of the flag. The Flag Code provides rules for displaying the flag by citizens, organizations, and government bodies. Citizens can display the flag at home any day with dignity. The flag should be displayed from sunrise to sunset, and if at night, it must be illuminated.



Indian Emblem


The emblem originates from Emperor Ashoka's Mauryan Empire in the 3rd century BCE. The Lion Capital at Sarnath is where Buddha gave his first sermon. A depiction of the Lion Capital was adopted as the emblem of the Dominion of India in 1947 and later the Republic of India.



Indian national Bird


Cultural Significance: The peacock holds deep cultural and mythological importance in India. It's associated with various deities in Hinduism, notably Lord Krishna who is often depicted with a peacock feather in his crown. It's also the vehicle of Lord Murugan (Kartikeya).



Indian national Animal


It was designated as the national animal in April 1973, replacing the Asiatic Lion. The tiger's selection was based on its widespread presence across India and its representation of the nation's strength, agility, grace, and cultural significance. It is also important to note that India has a National Heritage Animal, which is the Indian Elephant (Elephas maximus indicus). This designation highlights the elephant's cultural and ecological significance in the country.



Indian National Tree


Its ever-spreading branches and aerial roots, which form new trunks, give the tree a seemingly endless lifespan. Represents strength and resilience: The tree's massive size and deep roots are symbolic of India's enduring nature. Cultural and spiritual importance: The Banyan is revered in Hinduism and is associated with various deities and spiritual practices, often referred to as the "wish-fulfilling tree" or "Kalpavriksha".
